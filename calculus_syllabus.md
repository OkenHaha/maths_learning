# Calculus Syllabus Structure

## Philosophy of the Curriculum

Calculus should be taught as:

> The mathematics of change, motion, accumulation, and relationships

instead of only:
- formulas
- symbolic manipulation
- memorization

The curriculum should gradually move from:
- intuition
- visualization
- motion
- patterns

towards:
- abstraction
- symbolic reasoning
- higher dimensional thinking

---

# Learning Progression

```text
Patterns
→ Change
→ Motion
→ Rates
→ Instantaneous behavior
→ Accumulation
→ Continuous systems
→ Multivariable thinking
```

Prepare learners for calculus level thinking.

## Topics

- Coordinate geometry

- Rates and ratios

# Important Teaching Principle

Teach:

> WHY before HOW

Example progression:
- motion intuition
- changing speed
- tangent line idea
- local linearity
- derivative intuition
- derivative notation
- derivative rules

instead of introducing formulas immediately.

---

# Important Design Suggestions

Calculus should heavily use:
- animations
- interactive graphs
- motion simulation
- zooming curves
- tangent visualizations
- accumulation visualizations
- area approximation tools

Visualization is essential for deep understanding.

---

# Most Conceptually Critical Modules

These require especially careful teaching:
- Limits
- Derivative intuition
- Integration intuition
- Fundamental theorem of calculus

These modules determine whether learners:
- truly understand calculus
or
- only memorize symbolic procedures
